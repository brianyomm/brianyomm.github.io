Ignite your Renaissance

I was part of an early-stage startup formally called Patron, now Musae. Our vision was to build a platform for creatives and patrons to discover, network, and book other creative professionals. In short, we offer free business tools to help creatives save time, make money, and acquire fresh new clients.

As one of the founding members I was required to wear many hats as possible. I coordinated and led all facets of design including: user task flows, visual, product, prototyping, and even the pitch deck. I also conducted user research using methods such as interviews, and surveys.

The Patron organization was truely amazing despite the fact the team worked remotely across the globe. We were artists, engineers, musicians, designers, writers, hackers, and makers who came together with one mission. We applied Agile methodology and tasks were tracked and assigned using Asana task management tool.

Challenges

This being my first UI/UX role there were many things that needed to be self-taught. In retrospect, having a industrial design background helped. The entire process was the similar when comparing ID and UI/UX. From research, ideation, concepts, prototyping, and testing. Being one of the founding members, there were no guidelines or direct path for me to take. I considered this a plus since I enjoy experimentation and failing to move forward.

The product itself had alot of difficulties to overcome. For starters, we were located all around the globe who had full-time jobs. Overcoming time differences and setting a schedule that suited everyone was more difficult than anticipated.

Designing for two user types 'Creatives' and 'Patrons' was by far the biggest challenege. Where to draw the line would only be achieved by testing. But with no user-base, we relied on competitive analysis.

Goals

  • 1. Formulating a design language and choosing the right tools.

  • 2. Design a homepage for both Creatives and Patron with curated creative content.

  • 3. Design a signup process that caters to both Creatives and Patrons.

  • 4. Design Creative & Patron Profiles where users can curate a portfolio, display a bio, show off their creative service, read reviews about the creative.

  • 5. Design a dashboard that allows user's to have full control over settings, add creative services, booking features, and reviews.

  • 6. Growth hacking with 0 budget using social media and scraping tools.

  • 7. User testing our platform for insight.

  • 8. Rinse & repeat.

  • 9. Create a pitch deck to obtain dough to pay for caffiene + beer.

foundation + Tools

There were a couple of design related items that needed work. One major one being was a lack of design language. So a decision was made to define our design language by adopting Angular Material to achieve a simple but proven design library. From breakpoints to element placements, everything was previously tested for optimal usability.

During the early stages wireframes were designed in Adobe Illustrator and UXPin... So I suggested we start using Sketch. Nightmares were averted and countless hours were saved after I created our design library in Sketch. Adding uniform elements with a simple copy/paste sure as hell beat rebuilding in Illustrator. With right tools we proceeded to move forward.

Research & Analysis

We did not have a direct competitor but there were other platforms that essentially had similar features. A few of them being Airbnb, Taskrabbit, Patreon, Ello, and Twitch. We collected designs on how each platform introduced each feature to their audience and how they balanced the two user cases on a single page. We learned that in most cases the main site caters towards the Patron (in our case) to able to consume the content. And a separate page that explains how the platform works for content creators. This way the Patron will be delighted by services provided by Creatives, and the Creatives will be inspired by other Creatives.

In terms of design, AngularJS provides an excellent UI framework based on Google Material Design. With this extensive library, we are able to tailor each module to our liking. Then we compared our modules with existing designs and made adjustments if necessary

Identity

Our previous logo was clearly an afterthought shadowed by the development of the site. So I pushed the importance of a truely unique logo. Creative content is a personal experience for everyone, from artists creating it all the way to Patrons admiring it. We wanted to capture the essence of the arts by being a blank canvas to be dynamic. This way we are able to incorporate other forms, colors, and patterns to work in tandem with other creative content.

Homepage

Our goal was to create a page with dynamic content to capture our audience within 190 seconds (Global average time on site). Slow motion videos always do the trick. Cards with slight animation supported by descriptive text and a CTA to signup.
