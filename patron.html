Intro

I was part of an early-stage startup formally called Patron, now Musae. Our vision was to build a platform for creatives and patrons to discover, network, and book other creative professionals. In short, we offer free business tools to help creatives save time, make money, and acquire fresh new clients.

My job as one of the founding members was to wear many hats as possible in all things design and acquiring new users. I led the designs for most of what is shown on the web-app.

The Patron organization was truely amazing despite the fact the team worked remotely across the globe. We were artists, engineers, musicians, designers, writers, hackers, and makers who came together with one mission. We were an Agile team and work was tracked and assigned using Asana task management tool.

Design Language + Tools

When I joined Patron there were a couple of design related items that needed work. First one being a lack of design language. So a decision was made to define our design language by adopting Google Material Design to achieve a simple but proven design library. During the early stages wireframes were designed in Adobe Illustrator... So I suggested we start using Sketch. Nightmares were averted and countless hours were saved after creating a design library in Sketch. Adding uniform elements with a simple copy/paste sure as hell beat rebuilding in Illustrator.
